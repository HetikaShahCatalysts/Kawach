How the SOP supports action
It converts field observations into a structured risk profile, routes cases to the appropriate pathway, clarifies role-wise action, and supports consistent follow-up until the child is safer and better supported.
Start with the child. Let the assessment guide the pathway.
This tool is for CWPC members, frontline workers, programme teams and supervisors who need a structured way to observe risk, document vulnerability, and choose safer next steps.
It does not replace statutory authorities, police, CWC, DCPU, CMPO, Labour Department, or Child Helpline 1098. It helps field teams slow down, assess consistently, and escalate when needed.

Assessment Logic and User Guide
Visible documentation of scoring, pathway activation, overrides and monitoring
How the Kawach assessment works
This page documents the logic behind the current framework for field teams and supervisors.
1. Risk scoring
The worker first selects household, child, community and protective factors. Household + child + community produce the raw vulnerability score; protective factors are subtracted in the adjusted total shown to the worker.
2. Observation scoring
Observations are grouped into broad generic field buckets rather than CM / CL / CT labels. Each item still carries an internal backend tag for Regulated Labour, Prohibitive/Bonded Labour, Child Marriage, Child Trafficking, Substance Use or Migration Safety.
3. Pathway activation
A pathway opens when any critical trigger for that pathway is selected. If no critical trigger is selected, the same pathway opens when its pathway score is 5 or above. More than one pathway can open together, and the user can manually select additional pathways before Step 4.
4. Manual pathway selection
The score is advisory. If the worker believes the child is unsafe, coercion is present, the family may flee, or the situation may deteriorate, the worker can select the appropriate pathway directly in Step 3 before continuing to Step 4.
5. Protective factor rule
Protective factors now sit in Step 1. They carry negative scores and reduce the adjusted total score, but they cannot cancel critical triggers or pathway-specific escalation.
6. Pathway-specific escalation
Police/FIR escalation appears only inside the relevant statutory pathway step. Follow the pathway-specific instruction shown in Step 4 rather than using a generic escalation shortcut.

Phase 5 — Monitoring Plan
Step 5 of 7 · Monitoring guidance
Monitoring Guidance
This is a generic monitoring page. Pathway-specific monitoring details can be added later without changing the overall page structure.
1. Assign responsibility
Assign one CWPC/frontline focal person for each active case. Record who is responsible, date of next visit, and escalation contact.
2. Track child safety and traceability
At every visit, verify where the child is, who they are with, whether they are safe, and whether there are new signs of coercion, violence, movement, marriage or work.
3. Track education and daily routine
Check school/Anganwadi/skilling attendance, peer interaction, play/rest, health access and whether work or household duties are increasing.
4. Track family and service linkages
Review whether scheme linkage, counselling, livelihood support, documentation, health or legal support is moving forward. Record pending blockers.
5. Escalate on warning signs
Reopen or escalate immediately if risk rises, the child goes missing, family stops cooperating, marriage/work/migration plans restart, or violence/abuse is reported.
Phase 5 — Case Closure Principles
Step 6 of 7 · Status model, criteria, and reopening triggers
Case Closure Principles
Case closure in Kawach does not mean poverty ended or vulnerability disappeared. It means the immediate protection risk has reduced, the child is connected to protective systems, and community monitoring continues.
✓
Core principle: A case should move toward closure only when: immediate harm has reduced · child is safe and traceable · family has received support · protective systems are functioning · follow-up confirms reduced recurrence risk.
Case Status Model
Status 01
Active Case
Immediate risk exists. All interventions continue without pause.
Marriage likely, child still working, child missing
Violence or coercion ongoing
Family refusing intervention
Child untraceable or disconnected from support
Status 02
Stabilised Case
Immediate danger reduced. Vulnerability still present. Any 5 of 10 conditions must be met.
Child safe and traceable
Connected to education or skilling
Family counselling completed
Scheme and livelihood linkage initiated
CWPC focal person assigned and active
Follow-up visits conducted per schedule
Child emotionally stable and expressing safety
Peer support or child collective active
No immediate repeat risk observed at last visit
Community and system stakeholders informed and engaged
Status 03
Closed with Monitoring
All mandatory conditions met plus any 2 additional. Routine monitoring continues.
All 8 mandatory conditions met (Immediate risk addressed/prevented, Child currently safe, Child connected with education/skilling/support, Family informed/counselled, Follow-up completed for required period, No active repeat warning signs, CWPC monitoring responsibility assigned, Documentation completed in Panchayat Diary/register)
Plus any 2 of these (Scheme benefits received, Livelihood support functional, Child attending school regularly, Child emotionally stable, Child linked to peer collective, Community support active, System stakeholders aware)
CWPC monitoring responsibility formally assigned
Status 04
Closed — System Takeover
Statutory system is primary case holder. CWPC continues in a supporting role.
FIR registered, CWC inquiry active, child in CCI
Court proceedings ongoing, Labour Dept handling rescue
CWPC role: monitor wellbeing, support reintegration, prevent stigma, alert if new risk

Case Reopening Triggers
!
A closed or stabilised case must be immediately reopened if any of the following are observed:
—Marriage discussions restart
—Child returns to exploitative labour
—Child goes missing
—Trafficking concerns reappear
—School dropout resumes
—Violence or abuse reported
—Migration risk increases sharply
—Family stops cooperation with CWPC
